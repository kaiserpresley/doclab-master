Understanding Sickle Cell Anemia: Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis

Sickle cell anemia is a genetic blood disorder that affects the shape and function of red blood cells. In this article, we will discuss the causes, symptoms, and diagnosis of sickle cell anemia.

Causes:

Sickle cell anemia is caused by a mutation in the HBB gene, which provides instructions for making hemoglobin. Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout the body. The mutation causes the hemoglobin to form into an abnormal shape, which leads to the characteristic sickle shape of the red blood cells. Sickle cell anemia is an inherited condition, which means that it is passed down from parents to their children.

Symptoms:

The symptoms of sickle cell anemia can vary from person to person. Some common symptoms include:

  • Anemia or decreased number of red blood cells and oxygen
  • Irritability or fussiness in babies
  • Severe fatigue or tiredness
  • Frequent episodes of pain, also known as crises, in different parts of the body
  • Swelling of hands and feet, associated with pain
  • Frequent infections
  • Delayed growth noted in children and adults
  • Vision problems

Diagnosis:

Sickle cell anemia can be diagnosed through a blood test. Prenatal screening may be performed using amniotic fluid, and newborn screening is performed by a heel prick test. The doctor may also ask about family history and perform a physical exam to check for symptoms like enlargement of the spleen and pain.

Conclusion:

Sickle cell anemia is a genetic blood disorder that affects the shape and function of red blood cells. It is caused by a mutation in the HBB gene, which provides instructions for making hemoglobin. The symptoms of sickle cell anemia can vary from person to person, and it can be diagnosed through a blood test. If you or someone you know is exhibiting symptoms of sickle cell anemia, seek medical attention immediately.
